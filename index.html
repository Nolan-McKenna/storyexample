Hartford Area Habitat for Humanity Homes Generated Over $850,000 in Property Taxes in 2020

by Nolan McKenna

Last updated on 12/2/2021

for Data Visualization for All
with Prof. Jack Dougherty
Trinity College, Hartford CT, USA

Story

For my data build, I was tasked with finding out how much property tax revenue Hartford Area Habitat for Humanity homes generated for local governments in 2020.

Finding this information out is important because it shows how along with helping out families by providing affordable and safe housing, the organization also aids local governments by providing them with thousands of dollars in additional property taxes.

Property taxes often go back into the community by providing public education, improving infrastructure, and providing public safety, so any increase in them will help the community develop.

Since 1989, Hartford Area Habitat for Humanity has been providing this type of assistance to local families and governments. To date, they have built or renovated over 250 homes. Most recently in 2020, we found that the organization’s properties generated a total of $899,817 in total tax revenue for 15 different communities.

However, in the data that was provided by Hartford Area Habitat for Humanity, there were 18 addresses whose property tax records could not be found. Some of these addresses didn’t appear to exist, while others had homes there. Considering the unknown property taxes from these addresses, it’s safe to say that the organization actually generated over $900,000 dollars for local governments in 2020.

In order to gather property tax data and reveal these findings, Hartford Area Habitat for Humanity, Jack Dougherty, Nassar, Myri, Emma, and myself all collaborated heavily. We utilized various government property tax databases (see photo) to find the 2020 property tax generated by each Habitat home.
