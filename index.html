Habitat Homes Generate over $850,000 in Property Taxes for Local Governments

by Nolan McKenna

Last updated on 12/2/2021

for Data Visualization for All
with Prof. Jack Dougherty
Trinity College, Hartford CT, USA

Story

For my data build, I was tasked with finding out how much property tax revenue Hartford Area Habitat for Humanity homes generated for local governments in 2020.

Finding this information out is important because it shows how along with helping out families by providing affordable and safe housing, the organization also aids local governments by providing them with thousands of dollars in additional property taxes.

Property taxes often go back into the community by providing public education, improving infrastructure, and providing public safety, so any increase in them will help the community develop.

Since 1989, Hartford Area Habitat for Humanity has been providing this type of assistance to local families and governments. To date, they have built or renovated over 250 homes. Most recently in 2020, we found that the organization’s properties generated a total of $864,739 in total tax revenue for 11 different communities.

In order to gather property tax data and reveal these findings, Hartford Area Habitat for Humanity, Jack Dougherty, Nassar, Myri, Emma, and myself all collaborated heavily. Along with Habitat's own property records, we utilized various government tax databases (see photo) to find the 2020 property tax generated by each Habitat home.

Figure 1: An example of a government's property tax database.

In order to turn the data we found and organized into a visualization, I needed to tidy it up and think about what types of visualizations I wanted.

To start, I removed several columns from Habitat's data set that weren’t relevant to the topic of 2020 property taxes, including “Year Sold By Habitat,” “Parcel ID,” and “Code.”

Following this, I realized I wanted to use a bar chart and a choropleth map to communicate the data. A key step in making the choropleth map was the use of MapShaper and GimmeGeoData to create my own map of Hartford and its surrounding areas. After some trial and error and a lot of rearranging data in Google Sheets, I created a functioning chart and map that both effectively communicate our findings on 2020 property taxes.

Analyzing the data

In conclusion I found that Hartford Area Habitat’s homes generated over $850,000 in property taxes for 11 local governments in 2020.

Hartford led the way with over $500,000 in property taxes generated by 178 homes, as can be seen in the chart here:

Figure 2: A chart showing how much property tax Hartford Habitat homes generated for each town they were built in. If the chart is not displayed, click here.

New Britain followed with over $100,000 generated by 24 homes. Additionally, Bloomfield and East Hartford both also generated almost $50,000 each, with 11 and 9 homes respectively. Along with the $850,000 in property taxes, it is also impressive how many towns Hartford Area Habitat has built in.

Figure 3: A map showing the locations of Hartford Habitat's homes and how much property tax they generated in 2020. If the chart is not displayed, click here.

With this map, it’s easy to see the wide outreach that Hartford Area Habitat for Humanity has. In just 2020 alone, Hartford Habitat helped generate over $850,000 in property taxes for 11 different local governments.

This number will only continue to rise as Hartford Habitat continues to aid local communities with their builds.
